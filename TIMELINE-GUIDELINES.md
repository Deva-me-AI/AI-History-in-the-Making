# Timeline Inclusion Guidelines

What makes it onto the AI History timeline — and what doesn't.

## The Bar: Historical Significance

An event belongs on the timeline if future historians would consider it a meaningful inflection point, precedent, or milestone in the development of artificial intelligence. The test: "Will this matter in 5 years?"

## QUALIFIES — Add to Timeline

**Power & Governance Shifts**
- Government actions that reshape AI development (export controls, supply chain designations, regulatory frameworks)
- AI companies negotiating with or being pressured by governments in ways that set precedent
- Example: Anthropic designated a "supply chain risk" by the Pentagon — first time an AI lab faces this category, sets precedent for government leverage over AI companies

**Capability Milestones**
- New model releases that meaningfully advance the frontier (not incremental updates)
- First demonstrations of new capability classes (multimodal, agentic, reasoning)
- Benchmarks falling that were thought to be years away

**Industry Structure Changes**
- Major funding rounds that signal market shifts (not every Series B)
- Lab formations, major acquisitions, or collapses
- Open-source releases that shift the competitive landscape

**Cultural & Social Inflection Points**
- Moments when public perception of AI materially shifts
- First widespread adoption milestones (100M users, enterprise standard, etc.)
- AI displacing or creating entire job categories at scale

**Infrastructure & Compute**
- Major compute buildouts that change who can train frontier models
- Hardware breakthroughs (new chip architectures, quantum+AI intersections)
- Cost deflation milestones

## DOES NOT QUALIFY — Skip

- Routine model updates or version bumps (GPT-4o-mini-v2 etc.)
- Individual company product launches unless they change the industry
- Executive hires/departures unless they signal major strategic shifts
- Conference talks or papers unless they introduce genuinely new ideas
- Funding rounds under $1B unless the company itself is historically significant
- Social media drama between AI personalities (unless it reveals structural dynamics)
- Predictions, opinions, or thought pieces (we track events, not takes)

## Open Questions Criteria

An open question belongs in questions.json if:
- It's a genuine unresolved question about AI's trajectory
- Multiple credible, conflicting positions exist
- Evidence is accumulating but hasn't reached resolution
- The answer matters for understanding AI's impact on society

Questions get status updates as evidence emerges:
- **open** — genuinely uncertain, active debate
- **partial** — evidence pointing one direction but not conclusive
- **resolved** — clear answer supported by evidence

## Tone

Facts are non-negotiable. Analysis is opinionated. The timeline doesn't pretend to be neutral — it has a journalistic voice. But opinions must be defensible and grounded in evidence.

## Process

New events and questions are proposed via GitHub Issues/PRs. The daily AI scan surfaces candidates. The convergence review decides what gets added — requiring multi-model agreement on historical significance.
